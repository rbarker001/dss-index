At scale — 75 Kansas City SNFs, then all 15,000 US SNFs — this relationship either holds or it doesn't, and the database is what lets you test it empirically rather than assert it.
-- Average staffing by exposure level across all assessments
SELECT exposure_level,
  ROUND(AVG(staffing_hprd), 2) AS avg_hprd,
  ROUND(AVG(nursing_turnover_pct), 1) AS avg_turnover,
  COUNT(*) AS facilities
FROM assessment
GROUP BY exposure_level
ORDER BY avg_hprd DESC;

Which DSS domain appears most often?

The dominant domain. F-755, F-658, F-740/741, and staffing all map here. High recognition risk — awareness-change presentations are systematically misattributed to dementia fluctuation.

Same conditions as Domain 3 — these conditions span both. Behavioral seizure presentations are categorized as behavioral incidents, not neurological events.
At scale, this becomes a research question: If Domain 3 and 4 dominate across all Kansas City SNFs, does that hold in St. Louis? In rural Missouri? Nationally? That's an empirical finding about where the misattribution problem concentrates — publishable, citable, and the kind of thing that turns your framework into a standard.
-- Most common DSS domain in active conditions, by city
SELECT f.region, c.dss_domain, COUNT(*) AS count
FROM condition c
JOIN assessment a ON c.assessment_id = a.assessment_id
JOIN facility f ON a.facility_id = f.facility_id
WHERE c.classification != 'Not Identified'
  AND c.dss_domain IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY f.region, c.dss_domain
ORDER BY f.region, count DESC;

-- Avg recognized conditions by ownership type
SELECT a.ownership_type,
  ROUND(AVG(a.conditions_recognized), 1) AS avg_recognized,
  ROUND(AVG(a.staffing_hprd), 2) AS avg_hprd,
  COUNT(*) AS n
FROM assessment a
GROUP BY a.ownership_type
ORDER BY avg_recognized DESC;

Which conditions always appear together?
When F-658 (professional standards) is Recognized, what else is usually Recognized alongside it? Co-occurrence patterns reveal whether risk is isolated or systemic.

Pattern visible now: F-658 and F-740/741 never appear alone — every facility that has one has the other. F-689 appears in isolation (Prairie Village: F-689 Potential, but F-755 and F-740/741 Not Identified). This suggests F-658 + F-740/741 is a syndrome, not two independent conditions. At 15,000 facilities, you can test whether that co-occurrence is statistically reliable — which would make it a named risk cluster in your framework.
-- Facilities where F-658 is Recognized but F-740/741 is Not Identified
SELECT f.name, a.exposure_level
FROM facility f
JOIN assessment a ON f.facility_id = a.facility_id
JOIN condition c658 ON c658.assessment_id = a.assessment_id
  AND c658.source_citation_value = 'F-658'
  AND c658.classification = 'Recognized'
JOIN condition c740 ON c740.assessment_id = a.assessment_id
  AND c740.source_citation_value = 'F-740/F-741'
  AND c740.classification = 'Not Identified';
